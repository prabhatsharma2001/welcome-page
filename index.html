Story 01 · Manufacturing & Cement · Finance

The Problem

Every month-end, the CFO walked into the board meeting knowing one number: EBITDA was off. But he could not explain why — not with confidence, not in the room, not without calling three people and waiting a day for a reconciled Excel file.

SAP held the GL. The budget lived in Excel. Plant operations sat in a MIS nobody had connected. Freight costs came by email. Energy costs came from a separate report. Five people. Three days. One number — with no explanation for why it moved.

The CMD asked "why did margin fall despite revenue growth?" The honest answer was: we don't know yet. That answer had stopped being acceptable.

DoD connected SAP Datasphere, plant MIS, Excel budget, sales dispatch, freight, and energy data into one decision layer. A KPI tree decomposed EBITDA into drivers — revenue, volume, mix, freight, energy, fixed cost absorption — each wired to its source. The CFO now asks the question and gets a driver-wise answer, with source lineage, before the close meeting starts.

The board conversation shifted from "what is the number?" to "what do we do about it?"
